There is an old latin proverb, repetitio est mater studiorum, which means repetition is the mother of learning.

The proverb may be old but it still holds true; we learn the alphabet by repeating it over and over and we learn to ride a bike by falling and getting up again until we have mastered riding it.

Learning a new language is no different, we must listen to, speak, read and write the words of the language thousands of times before becoming fluent.

Actually, to tell the truth, there's no way around the "thousands of time" bit. There are no silver bullets. You have to work hard to learn a new language!
